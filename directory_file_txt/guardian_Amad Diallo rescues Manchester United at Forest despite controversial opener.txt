Title: Amad Diallo rescues Manchester United at Forest despite controversial opener

Summary: <p>Sean Dyche argued football’s lawmakers should consider expanding the reach of the video assistant referee system after Nottingham Forest conceded from a controversial corner for the second successive match. His side came close to winning but Dyche was left angered after Manchester United scored the opener from a corner awarded after the assistant referee Akil Howson, on the far side of the pitch, flagged for a corner despite inconclusive replays showing it was unclear whether the Forest full-back Nicolò Savona kept the ball in play.</p><p>Ruben Amorim was disappointed he could not celebrate his first anniversary in charge of Manchester United with victory and Dyche was again left baffled by the officials. Dyche was booked for his protests in defeat <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/football/2025/oct/26/bournemouth-nottingham-forest-premier-league-match-report">last weekend at Bournemouth</a>, where Marcus Tavernier scored directly from a corner, and here Casemiro headed in one taken by Bruno Fernandes to earn United a first-half lead. Forest rallied, scoring twice in 102 second-half seconds, but Amad Diallo’s stunning volley ensured the post-match discourse was dominated by the award of a 33rd-minute corner.</p> <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/football/2025/nov/01/nottingham-forest-manchester-united-premier-league-match-report">Continue reading...</a>
