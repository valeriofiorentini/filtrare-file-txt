Title: Rachel Reeves considers 20% tax on assets of people deciding to leave UK

Summary: <p>Plans for ‘settling-up charge’ predicted to raise £2bn and would follow policy in place among most G7 countries</p><p>Rich people quitting the UK could be required to pay a 20% tax on their business assets as part of plans reportedly being considered by the chancellor, Rachel Reeves.</p><p>The Treasury has drawn up plans for a “settling-up charge” on assets; a move that would bring the UK into line with most other G7 nations and raise a predicted £2bn for the public coffers, according to the Times.</p> <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2025/nov/01/rachel-reeves-considers-20-tax-on-assets-of-people-deciding-to-leave-uk">Continue reading...</a>
