Title: Spain expresses regret over ‘injustice’ suffered by Mexico’s Indigenous people during conquest

Summary: <p>Acknowledgment shows shift in tone after six years of diplomatic spats over colonial-era abuses</p><p>Spain has acknowledged and expressed regret over the “pain and injustice” suffered by the Indigenous people of Mexico during its conquest of the Americas, heralding a shift in tone after six years of diplomatic spats over the abuses of the colonial period.</p><p>In March 2019, Mexico’s then president, <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/world/andres-manuel-lopez-obrador">Andrés Manuel López Obrador</a> wrote to King Felipe VI and Pope Francis, who was then the leader of the world’s Roman Catholics, <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/world/2019/mar/25/mexico-demands-spain-apology-colonialism-obrador">urging them to apologise</a> for the “massacres and oppression” of colonialism and the conquest.</p> <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/nov/01/spain-expresses-regret-over-injustice-suffered-by-mexicos-indigenous-people-during-conquest">Continue reading...</a>
